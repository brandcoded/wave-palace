# WavePalace — Master Brand Brief

> Copy this entire document into any AI (Claude, ChatGPT, Midjourney, Canva AI, Adobe Firefly) or hand it to a designer as a creative brief.

---

## What WavePalace Is

A platform where DJs, artists, and curators build permanent looping audio channels that play inside VRChat virtual worlds and link anywhere on the web. Creator-owned channels — always-on, visual (cinematic backdrops), with the creator's name in every player overlay.

Not a streaming service. Not metaverse radio. Not a startup pitch.
A creator-owned channel platform. Channels run 24/7 without anyone being live.

---

## What It Is Not

- Metaverse radio (that trope is dead — do not use the phrase)
- Internet radio in a VR skin
- A centrally programmed network
- Anything that requires hype to survive
- A concept — it is a working product

---

## Brand Personality

Understated confidence. Dark, cinematic, immersive, music-forward.

Reference points: a well-curated music venue, a late-night record store, a cinematic music documentary. Permanent, atmospheric, quality-forward.

**Yes:** Luxury editorial (Dazed, i-D, The Face), late-night music venue interior, architectural brutalism + soft light, cinematic music documentary.

**No:** EDM festival / neon cyberpunk, SaaS startup dashboard, VR gaming UI (health bars, crosshairs), lo-fi YouTube anime girl aesthetic, corporate radio vibes.

### Typography

- Display / wordmark: clean grotesque — Inter, Sohne, or equivalent
- No serif for primary identity
- Weights: Light (300) for atmospheric moments, Regular (400) for body, Medium (500) for UI, Semibold (600) max for headlines
- Large white headlines on dark. Minimal copy. Let breathing room do the work.

### Graphic Motifs

- Subtle audio waveforms and frequency bar visualizations
- Frosted / glassy UI panels — light translucency, not heavy glow
- Architectural scale compositions — the "Palace" is real; feels like entering a space
- Implied motion, never chaotic — smooth, ambient, flowing
- No gradients, no neon glow, no heavy drop shadows

## Creator Personas (Who We Are Talking To)

### A1 — The Invisible Producer (Artist / Producer)
- Ages 20–34. Lo-fi, ambient, electronic, hip-hop beats.
- Platforms: Spotify, SoundCloud, Instagram, Reddit.
- Problem: Tracks sit silent on DSPs. Algorithm is unpredictable. No passive discovery path.
- What works: Discovery without promotion effort. Attribution on every play (name on screen). Drives Spotify listens, doesn't replace them.
- Pitch line: "Your music, on the dial, in every world that plays it."

### A2 — The Between-Sets DJ
- Ages 22–38. House, techno, DNB, chill, hybrid sets.
- Platforms: Mixcloud, SoundCloud, VRChat, Twitch, Discord.
- Problem: Mixes have no visual home. VRChat placement is technical. Audience resets between events.
- What works: Permanent presence between sets. VRChat reach without being live. Audience is there when you go live.
- Pitch line: "A live set lasts a night. A channel lasts."

### A3 — The Taste Architect (Curator / Selector)
- Ages 25–40. Mood-driven, genre-fluid, niche-obsessed.
- Platforms: Spotify playlists, Are.na, Substack, Twitter/X.
- Problem: Taste-making has no dedicated format. Playlists are buried. Posts disappear.
- What works: "The channel IS the format." No music production required. A permanent home for your taste.
- Pitch line: "You already know what belongs together. WavePalace gives that a URL."

### A4 — The Platform-Renter (Music Personality / Host)
- Ages 22–42. Radio-brained, community-first, brand-aware.
- Platforms: Twitch, YouTube, Instagram, VRChat events.
- Problem: Platform owns the audience. Always starting from zero. No independent "station" concept.
- What works: "Hosted by [Name]" on every listener's screen. Own the station, not just the show.
- Pitch line: "Your own station. No broadcast license required."

## Listener Personas (Who Listens)

### L1 — The World Builder (VRChat World Owner / Creator)
- Ages 18–35. Builds VRChat worlds in Unity.
- Problem: No curated ambient audio source. YouTube links go down. Generic lo-fi doesn't fit every world.
- They are the distribution channel. One popular world = hundreds of passive listeners.
- Message: "Drop a URL into your world. Curated, stable, matched to your aesthetic."

### L2 — The Ambient Dweller (VRChat Regular)
- Ages 16–30. Hangs out in music lounge worlds.
- Primary listener base. Organic word-of-mouth vector.

### L3 — The Ambient Focus Listener (Web, non-VRChat)
- Ages 20–40. WFH, deep work, aesthetic-driven.
- Natural web player audience. Tired of lo-fi YouTube.
- Message: "Like lo-fi YouTube — but the music has somewhere to come from."

## Key Objections to Address

### "Will WavePalace hurt my DSP streams or YouTube views?"
WavePalace is not a DSP competitor. It's background radio in a venue that's always open. VRChat listeners are not choosing between WavePalace and Spotify — they're in a world, the music is ambient, they didn't open an app to listen. This is discovery exposure, not consumption that displaces DSP plays. The "Artist — Track Title" display sends people to Spotify, not away from it. No exclusivity — nothing prevents releasing or monetizing anything else.

Frame: "Think of it as your music in a venue that's always open. The venue doesn't take your Spotify streams — it sends people to them."

Do NOT claim WavePalace contributes to DSP royalties — it doesn't. This is exposure and reach, not a revenue stream (yet).

### "Does a channel compete with my event worlds?"
VRChat events succeed on presence and social energy, not content scarcity. A looping channel is not the same as being in the room with a DJ and a crowd reacting in real time. The channel is the between-events presence that keeps the host's audience warm. When Slice 4 ships (live events), the channel URL flips live — the people who already have it in their world are the guaranteed warm audience.

Frame: "The channel keeps you on the dial between events. When you go live, the audience is already there."

## The VRChat Angle (Shared Across All Personas)

Most music platforms don't know VRChat exists. WavePalace does. VRChat has millions of active users in worlds that loop ambient audio 24/7 with no curation layer. There is no competition for that attention. A WavePalace channel in one popular world can reach hundreds of concurrent listeners with zero promotion effort. That's the hook no Spotify or SoundCloud pitch can match — passive, ambient, in-world reach to an audience that's already there and already listening.

---

## Differentiation from "Metaverse Radio" Predecessors

Previous attempts (VR Vibes et al.) were concepts that needed believers. WavePalace is a tool that needs users. The structural difference:

- They announced before shipping. WavePalace ships first, announces second.
- They required a programming team. WavePalace channels are creator-owned.
- They relied on hype cycle momentum. WavePalace is useful without hype.
- They were media companies. WavePalace is a platform.

**Proof over promise:** Every outreach should arrive with evidence — a live channel URL, a screenshot of a channel playing in a world, a real creator's name on the dial. The absence of hype language is itself a differentiator.
